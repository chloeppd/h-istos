H • ISTOS

A study of parallel cultures inside history's tapestry

Inspired by the greek word for web or woven fabric, H•ISTOS is a digital tool for the crossdisciplinary exploration of interwoven narratives, cultures, and ideas.

  • Get Started

Introduction

Despite the dynamic and complex nature inherent in the field of historical study, traditional teaching practices tend to systematically reduce historical topics into binary, rigid narratives, often neglecting essential elements for a more comprehensive and profound understanding.

This projects is a prototype for a digital tool that encourages a more meaningful way of studying history through the lens of culture. With the use of both artistic and historiographic sources, narrative storytelling and the interaction with 3D assets, it aims to reconstruct traditional historical narratives by emphasizing not on the unique features of ancient regions cultures and customs, but how their interactions were central both for shaping their identity, and for fostering the international exchange of ideas.

The first chapter of this project serves as an introduction to cultural influences between the West and the East in the antiquity, focusing on the exchanges between the empires of Greece and Persia

Silk Road

The infamous Silk Road operated from the 2nd century BCE, spanning from China to Central Asia, India, Persia and the Roman Empire. Before its formal establishment, however, early trade networks between the East and the West had existed for many centuries, fostering the transmission of ideas, goods and technologies, as well as religious practices.

Project workflow

Tools and methods for constructing the current and future versions of the H • ISTOS project.

3D Modelling

3D design of cultural artefacts using reference pictures; texturing, sculpting, reconstruction of the model; scene creation using Blender

Storytelling

Design of a narrative that promotes engaging and effective learning using the website interface and -foreseeably- an interactive experience based on the 3D prototype

Project proposal

Further development includes the design of an interactive application with the 3D assets of each chapter, based on user curiosity and engagement, to inspect how cultural exchanges reflect on extant and reconstructed artefacts.

Following the proposed further development, the final outcome of the project would be a digital platform that hosts chapters with studies of cultural exchanges amongst ancient regions paired with a relevant interactive experience using 3D assets.

The website interface would serve as a repository of different historical and cultural sources for a crossdisciplinary study of each chapter, presented by means of storytelling techniques to inspire curiosity. The accompanying digital application would allow for a more meaningful exploration of the chapters' topics, based on the interaction with cultural objects, narrative methods, and/or care and empathy, depending on the nature of the topic.. of
